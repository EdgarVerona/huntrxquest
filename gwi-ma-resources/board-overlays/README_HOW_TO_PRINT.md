## Printing Overlays

I've broken up each A4/letter-sized page of content that requires printing for inserts, to make it easier for people to print-and-play.

If you've got the cash and time, you could use the original source images to instead print to a larger poster or some other solution, but for scrubs like me this is probably the best cost-to-effort ratio for playing the game.

**To print the inserts:**

1. pick the year for your board (1989/1990 or 2020), and go into that subfolder.  
     
2. Go into the a4\_print\_ready/letter\_print\_ready subfolder under that (depending on if you're using A4 or letter sized paper).  At the moment, 2020 just happened to work such that all the sheets should print fine whether A4 or letter, but I made a separate folder for it anyways in case that changes and just to make it less confusing for people.  
     
3. Take all of the files in that chosen subfolder, and print them through an application that will honor its built-in resolution settings and for the paper matching the folder you chose.

**InfranView is my preferred app to do print images with “true” size on Windows**, but you might have another preference.  It is absolutely critical that it keeps the scale the same as the built-in resolution settings of the images however, or they won't work as proper overlays.

Some are broken up into multiple files, with "\_AxB.jpg" as their file extension: those are overlays that had to be split over multiple A4 pages.  You will have to line them up, cut them, and tape them together to form the larger overlay.

There are also some that are duplicates but labeled "second\_sheet" or "third\_sheet".  That's just because you need to print out multiple of them, and I wanted to make it as simple as "print every image in this folder".
